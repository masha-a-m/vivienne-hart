Latest Novel · 2024

The Amber Hours

When Clara Vane returns to the Norfolk coast after thirty years, she expects to find the house she grew up in. What she finds instead is a version of her past that has been quietly, methodically rearranged.

The Amber Hours is a novel about the stories families tell instead of the truth, and the particular grief of understanding too late.

"I am interested in what gets said in the margins — the pauses, the evasions, the things people almost tell each other."

Vivienne Hart was born on the Lizard Peninsula in Cornwall in 1979, the youngest of four children in a family of schoolteachers and one largely unacknowledged painter. She spent her twenties in London, working in publishing and writing two novels she has never shown to anyone.

Her debut, The Light We Buried, was published in 2018 to immediate critical acclaim, described by the Observer as "an extraordinary debut — a novelist fully formed." The novel was adapted for BBC Radio 4 in 2020 and has been taught in secondary schools across the UK.

Her second novel, Salt & Sorrow (2021), was shortlisted for the Baileys Women's Prize and longlisted for the Women's Prize for Fiction. Set across a single week on the Outer Hebrides, it is a novel about grief, silence, and the language of coastlines.

Her most recent novel, The Amber Hours, was published in spring 2024. She lives in Provence with her husband, a ceramicist, and a dog of unremarkable character but strong opinions about walks.
